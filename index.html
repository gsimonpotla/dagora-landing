In 2025, can't we do better?

Bullying

Two-time world debate champion Bo Seo saw the potential for the format of debate to be exploited and degraded, and he believes that the capacity to disagree well or poorly is within all of us.

Polarization

Our brains naturally form social bonds by grouping people based on shared values, but digital spaces disrupt this. Social media magnifies conflict, deepens polarization, and makes empathy harder by exposing users to extreme viewpoints.

Echo Chambers

Scientific analyzes demonstrate echo chambers on various social platforms such as Twitter, Reddit, Facebook and Gab.

Fake news

People are more likely to share novel, surprising, or emotionally charged information, even if it's false. And social media platforms incentivize habitual sharing, making users more likely to spread misinformation without critically evaluating it.

Censorship

Twitter suspended multiple journalists who covered Elon Musk, citing a new policy against sharing live location data. Critics argue this was a targeted act of censorship, as the suspensions lacked prior warning and disproportionately affected reporters critical of Musk.

Manipulation

Redditors are being manipulated by AI-powered bots in the r/changemyview subreddit, as revealed by controversial research at the University of Zurich. Using AI bots in the name of science is one thing, but what happens when bad actors get their hands on these highly charismatic LLMs?
